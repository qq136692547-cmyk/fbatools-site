How to Calculate Amazon FBA Profit in 2026: Step-by-Step Formula

Most Amazon sellers calculate profit by subtracting the referral fee and fulfillment cost, then calling it a day. The problem: Amazon charges at least seven different fees per unit in 2026, and skipping even two can overestimate your margin by 10 percentage points or more.

Here's the complete formula for Amazon FBA profit per unit, with real examples at $30 and $50 price points, covering every fee Amazon charges in 2026.

The Complete Formula

Amazon FBA profit per unit in 2026 is calculated as:

Net Profit = Sale Price − Referral Fee − FBA Fulfillment − Inbound Placement − Low-Inventory Fee − Storage − Aged Inventory − Returns Processing − COGS − Ad Spend

Each fee matters. A 2026 analysis by True Margin found that Amazon FBA fees typically eat 30-40% of a seller's revenue. Many sellers believe their margin is higher because they only track the two largest fees.

Fee 1: Referral Fee (8–20%)

Amazon charges a category-dependent referral fee on every sale. Key 2026 rates: Electronics 8%, Home & Kitchen 15%, Apparel 17%, Jewelry 20%, Tools 15%, Sports 15% (source: FBAlytic, 2026). Most standard categories are 15%. There's a $0.30 minimum fee per item.

Fee 2: FBA Fulfillment ($3.06+)

Updated January 15, 2026, FBA fulfillment fees range from $3.06 for small standard to over $75 for large bulky. A typical large standard item (8-12 oz) costs about $4.10 per unit. An additional 3.5% fuel and inflation surcharge applies to all base fulfillment fees since April 17, 2026 (source: Eightx, 2026). Products under $10 use the Low-Price FBA rate, about $0.86 cheaper per unit.

Fee 3: Inbound Placement ($0.30–$2.30)

Introduced in 2024 and updated in 2026, this fee applies when you choose the minimal shipment split option (shipping to one Amazon warehouse). Rates: $0.30 (small standard) to $2.30 (large bulky) per unit. Amazon-optimized splits are fee-free but require shipping to multiple locations.

Fee 4: Low-Inventory Fee ($0.32–$2.09)

Amazon charges this when your historical days of supply drops below 28 days at the FNSKU level. Rates range from $0.32 (small standard) to $2.09 (bulky). Expanded to bulky items in January 2026 (source: Seller Snap, 2026). Grocery products are newly exempt.

Fee 5: Monthly Storage

Standard-size storage: $0.78/cu ft (Jan–Sep), $2.40/cu ft (Oct–Dec). Oversize: $0.56–$1.30/cu ft. For a standard product with 0.5 cu ft of monthly storage, that's about $0.39–$1.20 per month.

Fee 6: Aged Inventory Surcharge

Units stored 181+ days incur additional fees that escalate at 365 days. This is one of the most commonly overlooked costs — a slow-moving SKU can lose 5-10% of its value to storage fees alone.

Fee 7: Returns Processing

Amazon charges a returns processing fee for products in apparel, shoes, jewelry, and luggage categories. Typically $2–$6 per returned unit. Even if your return rate is only 5%, this adds $0.10–$0.30 per unit sold.
