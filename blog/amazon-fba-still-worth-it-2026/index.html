Is Amazon FBA Still Worth It in 2026? Complete Cost Analysis

With rising fees, new surcharges, and increasing competition, many sellers are asking whether Amazon FBA is still worth starting in 2026. The short answer: yes, but the margin for error is smaller than ever.

Amazon FBA fees have evolved significantly over the past two years. New fees like the low-inventory fee and inbound placement fee have added layers of cost that didn't exist before. Here's a complete breakdown of what FBA actually costs in 2026 and whether it's still profitable.

The New Fees That Changed the Math

Two fees introduced since 2024 have significantly changed FBA profitability:

Low-inventory fee ($0.32-$2.09/unit): Expanded to bulky items in January 2026 (source: Seller Snap, 2026). This fee penalizes sellers who can't maintain 28 days of supply. It's designed to encourage consistent inventory levels, but it adds a meaningful cost for seasonal or unpredictable products.

Fuel and inflation surcharge (3.5%): Applied to all FBA fulfillment fees since April 17, 2026. This is a percentage-based surcharge that compounds with rising fulfillment costs.

When Amazon FBA Still Works

  • High-volume staple products: Items with consistent demand and predictable inventory needs avoid low-inventory fees
  • Categories with lower referral fees: Electronics (8%) and Automotive (12%) leave more room for profit than Apparel (17%)
  • Products over $30: Higher price points absorb the fixed-cost fees (fulfillment, placement) better than sub-$20 items
  • Search-driven categories: Amazon still dominates product search — if customers search for your product on Amazon first, FBA is your best option

When to Consider Alternatives

TikTok Shop's 6% fee structure is significantly cheaper for visual, impulse-buy products in the $20-$60 range. FBT fulfillment ($2.86-$3.58/unit) is comparable to FBA but without the aged-inventory surcharges or low-inventory fees.
