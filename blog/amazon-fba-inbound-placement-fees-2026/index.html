Amazon FBA Inbound Placement Fees 2026: What Sellers Actually Pay

Amazon's inbound placement fee is the most misunderstood cost in the FBA fee stack. It looks small per unit ($0.30-$2.30), but it applies to every single unit you send to Amazon — and unlike referral or fulfillment fees, it hits before the first sale.

How Inbound Placement Works

When you create a shipment in Seller Central, Amazon decides which fulfillment centers should receive your inventory. The inbound placement fee covers the cost of distributing your products across the network so customers get fast delivery. You can influence the fee by choosing how many destinations your shipment is split across.

2026 Fee Rates

Shipment TypeStandard-sizeOversize
Minimal split (4+ destinations)$0.30/unit$0.60/unit
Standard split (2-3 destinations)$0.60/unit$1.20/unit

The minimal split option sends your inventory to 4+ fulfillment centers. Amazon charges the lowest per-unit rate but requires you to create separate shipments to each location. The premium split sends everything to one warehouse — convenient, but 4-7× more expensive per unit.

The Hidden Trap: Small Shipments

Amazon charges proportionally more for small shipments. A single-box shipment of 6 units can incur a per-unit rate of $1.50-$2.50 even on the minimal split option, because the fixed logistics cost per destination is spread across fewer units. Consistently sending in volumes of 20+ units per SKU keeps the per-unit fee at the bottom of the rate range.

Strategies to Minimize Inbound Placement Fees

  • Always choose minimal split. The extra 10 minutes of prep time to create 4 shipments instead of 1 saves you $1.00-$1.70 per unit.
  • Ship 20+ units per SKU. Small shipments get penalized. Consolidate slower-moving products into larger, less frequent shipments.
  • Standard-size vs oversize matters. If your product is oversize by 1 inch in any dimension, the placement fee doubles. Consider packaging changes to stay within standard-size bounds.

What is the cheapest inbound placement option?

The minimal split option (4+ destinations) is the cheapest at $0.30/unit for standard-size products. It requires more work to create separate shipments but saves up to 85% compared to the single-destination option.

Does Amazon refund inbound placement fees?

No. Inbound placement fees are non-refundable once Amazon receives the shipment. If your shipment is lost or damaged in transit, file a reimbursement claim for the product value, but the placement fee is not recoverable.
