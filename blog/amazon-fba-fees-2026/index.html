Amazon FBA Fees in 2026: A Complete Breakdown

Every fee Amazon charges an FBA seller in 2026 — explained with examples and an interactive calculator at the end. Amazon's fee schedule has changed every year for the last five years, and 2026 is no different: two of the newest fees, the Inbound Placement Fee and the Low-Inventory Fee, can each take an extra $0.20–$0.40 per unit if you don't manage them. This post breaks down what every fee does, when it applies, and how to model it before you place a purchase order.

1. Referral fee (6%–15%)

The referral fee is a percentage of the total sale price (including shipping) charged on every item sold. Most consumer categories are 15%, but several are 8% (consumer electronics, computers), and a few have special rules.

  • Most categories: 15%
  • Consumer Electronics, Computers, Cameras, Cell Phones, Video Games, Video Game Consoles: 8%
  • Watches: $0.30 minimum (16% under $2, 15% over $1,500)
  • Amazon Device Accessories: 45%, capped at $0.30 per unit
  • Grocery: 8% on the first $3, 15% on the rest

Example: A $29.99 product in "Home & Kitchen" pays a $4.50 referral fee (15%).

2. FBA fulfillment fee (per unit)

Amazon's flat per-unit fee to pick, pack, ship, and provide customer service. Determined by the item's size tier and unit weight. The four main tiers (US, 2026):

  • Small Standard (≤6 oz, ≤10×8×2 in): $3.06 base + $0.20/lb over 0.25 lb
  • Large Standard (≤1 lb, ≤18×14×8 in): $3.83 base + $0.20/lb over 0.75 lb
  • Large Bulky (≤50 lb, ≤108×84×42 in): $9.61 base + $0.42/lb over 1.5 lb
  • Extra Large (≤150 lb): $78.58 base + $0.83/lb over 50 lb

3. Inbound Placement Fee (new in 2024, still in 2026)

Effective April 1, 2024. This fee replaced the previous FBA inbound shipping discounts. It is charged per unit you ship into Amazon's network, and the amount depends on your size tier and which placement program you choose:

  • Minimal (Amazon assigns FCs to your inventory): cheapest, sometimes $0 for small standard
  • Partial (you pick some FCs): middle fee
  • Amazon Partnered Carrier: a third tier between Minimal and Partial

For a 0.75 lb Large Standard item on Partial, the placement fee is roughly $0.21 per unit. Over 5,000 units that's $1,050 — meaningful but not catastrophic.

4. Low-Inventory Fee (new in 2024, still in 2026)

Also effective April 1, 2024. Amazon charges this when your historical days-of-supply falls below 28 days relative to recent demand. The fee is per unit sold and ranges from $0.32 (small standard) to $1.10 (extra large).

The fix: keep at least 4 weeks of forward inventory at all times for your best-selling SKUs. The penalty is more expensive than the carrying cost of extra inventory.

5. Monthly storage fee (per cubic foot)

Amazon charges by the volume of space your inventory occupies. Standard-size items pay $0.87/cubic foot/month from January through September, then $2.40/cubic foot/month from October through December — almost 3× more. Bulky items have a separate, lower schedule. Long-term storage surcharges apply to inventory over 365 days old.

6. Optional fees you'll also see

  • Removal / disposal fee — charged when you ask Amazon to return or destroy your inventory.
  • Return processing fee — for high-return-rate items in certain categories.
  • Aged-inventory surcharge — applies to inventory in FCs for more than 181 days (181–210 days: lower tier; 271+ days: highest tier).
  • Multi-channel fulfillment (MCF) fee — for FBA orders shipped to non-Amazon channels.

Putting it together

A 0.75 lb product sold for $29.99 with $6.50 COGS and $1.20 inbound shipping — in the "Home & Kitchen" category, non-Q4, with healthy inventory — earns about $13.60 net profit per unit (45% margin). Switch to Q4 storage, drop into a low-inventory situation, and the same product drops to about $11.50 (38% margin). Same product, two completely different outcomes.
