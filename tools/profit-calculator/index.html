How the Amazon FBA Profit Calculator Works

The Amazon FBA profit calculator combines Amazon's full fee schedule with your cost inputs to reveal true take-home profit per unit. You enter your sale price, category, size tier, product cost, and shipping costs. The tool calculates FBA fees (referral, fulfillment, storage, placement, low-inventory) and FBM fees (referral, outbound shipping) side by side, then subtracts everything from revenue to show net profit, margin, and ROI for both fulfillment methods. A winner declaration tells you which method earns more on your specific product.

Profit Breakdown

FBA profit is a chain of deductions that starts with your sale price and ends with what hits your bank account. Many sellers calculate profit as price minus product cost minus the 15% referral fee — then wonder why their actual settlement is lower. Frequently Asked Questions

What is a good FBA profit margin?

A solid FBA margin is 20%–30% after referral fees, fulfillment, storage, and product cost. Below 15% and you're vulnerable to fee hikes and return costs. Private label products often target 30%+ while wholesale resellers operate on thinner 10–15% margins with higher volume.

Does ROI include ad spend?

Yes. The calculator subtracts your Amazon Ads spend (entered as a percentage of sale) before computing ROI. This gives you a realistic return figure that accounts for what you pay to acquire customers — not just the fees Amazon deducts from your settlement.

How is break-even calculated?

Break-even is the sale price where net profit equals zero — all fees and costs covered, nothing left over. If the calculator shows $0 net profit at your current price, you've found break-even. Anything above that price is your profit margin. Test different prices to find your minimum viable listing price.

How does this compare to Seller Fulfilled Prime (SFP)?

SFP lets FBM sellers earn the Prime badge while self-fulfilling. This calculator's FBM side shows your profit without SFP fees. To model SFP, add the per-unit SFP fee (typically $2–$4) to your FBM outbound shipping cost. SFP can be more profitable than FBA on bulky items where fulfillment fees are high.
