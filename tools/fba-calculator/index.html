How Amazon's 2026 FBA fees work

Amazon charges sellers five main fees on every FBA sale. Together they typically take 30%–45% of your sale price — so an accurate calculator is essential before you place an order with a supplier.

  1. Referral fee — 6%–15% of sale price, depending on category.
  2. FBA fulfillment fee — A flat per-unit fee based on size tier and weight.
  3. Inbound placement fee — Effective April 2024. Charged per unit sent to Amazon based on how you split inventory across fulfillment centers.
  4. Low-inventory fee — Effective April 2024. Charged on standard-size units when historical inventory falls below 28 days of supply relative to recent demand.
  5. Monthly storage fee — Per cubic foot, doubled in Q4 (October–December).

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this calculator accurate for 2026 Amazon FBA fees?

Yes. The fee tables are taken from Amazon's official 2026 US Fee Schedule and updated for the Inbound Placement Fee and Low-Inventory Fee. Real Amazon statements can differ by ±2%.

Does Amazon take 15% referral fee on every category?

No. Most consumer categories are 15%, but Amazon Device Accessories are 45% (capped at $0.30 per unit), Consumer Electronics and Computers are 8%, and Grocery is a tiered 8%/15% split.

What is the Inbound Placement Fee?

Effective April 1 2024. It's a per-unit fee that varies by size tier. It is lowest when Amazon assigns fulfillment centers for you ("Minimal") and highest when you choose to ship to a single Amazon FC yourself.

What is the Low-Inventory Fee?

Effective April 1 2024. Charged per unit sold when your historical days-of-supply is below 28 days, relative to recent demand. It applies only to standard-size units.
