How the Amazon FBA Calculator Works

The Amazon FBA calculator takes your sale price, product category, size tier, and cost inputs, then applies Amazon's full 2026 fee schedule to show what you actually keep per unit. The tool calculates referral fees (8%–17% by category), FBA fulfillment fees (by size tier), inbound placement fees, low-inventory fees, and monthly storage costs. It also factors in your product cost and inbound shipping to reveal net profit, margin, and ROI. The result is a complete fee stack that matches what Amazon will deduct from your settlement — no surprises on payday.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is an FBA fee?

FBA fees are the charges Amazon deducts when it fulfills your orders. They include the referral fee (a commission on each sale), the fulfillment fee (picking, packing, and shipping), storage fees, and optional surcharges like low-inventory and aged-inventory fees. Together, they typically total 25–35% of your sale price.

How often are storage fees billed?

Amazon bills storage fees monthly, calculated based on your average daily inventory volume (cubic feet) during that month. Rates increase significantly from October through December for Q4 peak season. The calculator lets you set your warehouse months and Q4 toggle to estimate accurately.

What is the low-inventory fee?

The low-inventory fee is a surcharge Amazon applies when your FBA inventory supply falls below 28 days of historical demand. It's designed to discourage understocking. The fee varies by size tier and can add $0.15–$0.50+ per unit. Select “Low” in the Inventory level field to see its impact.

Does the calculator include the referral fee?

Yes. The referral fee is always included — it's Amazon's commission on every sale, ranging from 8% to 17% depending on category. It's the first deduction shown in the results panel, before fulfillment and storage fees are applied.

How accurate are the fee calculations?

The calculator uses Amazon's published 2026 fee schedule and is updated regularly. However, actual fees can vary slightly based on product dimensions, weight rounding, promotional waivers, and category-specific rules. Use it for planning and pricing decisions, then verify against your actual settlement statements.
